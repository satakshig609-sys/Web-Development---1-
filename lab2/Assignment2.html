Natural Disasters

A flood is the overflowing of water onto normally dry land, caused by excess rainfall, snowmelt, or dam failures, leading to significant damage to homes, infrastructure, and crops, and causing loss of life and community disruption

A drought is a prolonged period of below-average rainfall, resulting in a significant shortage of water that negatively impacts agriculture, ecosystems, and human life

An earthquake is the sudden, violent shaking of the Earth's surface, caused by the abrupt release of energy from the movement of tectonic plates along fault lines. This energy creates seismic waves that travel through the Earth and cause the ground to tremble.
